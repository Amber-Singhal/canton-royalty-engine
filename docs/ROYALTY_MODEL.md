# Canton Royalty Engine: Data Model and Workflows

This document outlines the business logic, data models (Daml templates), and core workflows for the Canton Royalty Engine. The engine is designed to automate the calculation and distribution of royalty payments for digital content in a transparent, atomic, and auditable manner on a Canton network.

## 1. Overview

The primary goal of the Canton Royalty Engine is to provide a trusted, automated system for content creators and rights holders to manage royalty splits and receive payments. It replaces manual accounting, reduces administrative overhead, and ensures that payments are distributed accurately and instantly according to pre-agreed terms.

The system is built around a set of core Daml smart contracts that represent the content, the royalty agreement, and the various parties involved.

## 2. Key Actors

The system defines several key roles, represented by `Party` on the ledger:

*   **Creator**: The original author or creator of the digital content. The Creator initiates the `ContentMaster` and the initial `RoyaltyAgreement`.
*   **Rights Holder**: Any party entitled to a share of the royalties. This can include the Creator, co-authors, publishers, investors, or other stakeholders.
*   **Licensee**: A third-party entity (e.g., a streaming service, digital storefront, or distributor) that is licensed to use the content and is responsible for reporting usage/revenue data.
*   **Operator**: The party operating the Royalty Engine service. The Operator may facilitate the creation of contracts but is not a signatory on the core agreements between creators and rights holders.

## 3. Core Daml Contracts

The logic is encapsulated in a few key Daml templates:

*   `ContentMaster`: Represents the unique digital asset (e.g., a song, an ebook, a video). It contains metadata like an ISIN, ISRC, or a unique hash, and points to the `RoyaltyAgreement` governing it.
*   `RoyaltyAgreement`: The central contract that governs the royalty distribution for a specific `ContentMaster`. It is signed by the Creator and contains the rules for distribution, including the payment cadence and the list of authorized Licensees.
*   `ShareAllocation`: A child contract to the `RoyaltyAgreement`. Each `ShareAllocation` contract represents a single Rights Holder's percentage stake in the royalties. The sum of all active `ShareAllocation` contracts for a given `RoyaltyAgreement` must always equal 100%.
*   `LicenseeAgreement`: An agreement between the Creator (or their agent) and a Licensee, authorizing the Licensee to submit usage reports for a specific `ContentMaster`.
*   `UsageReport`: A contract created by an authorized Licensee to report revenue generated by the content for a specific period. This contract contains the gross revenue amount that needs to be distributed.

## 4. Key Workflows

### 4.1. Asset Onboarding and Agreement Creation

1.  A **Creator** initiates the process by creating a `ContentMaster` contract on the ledger. This contract acts as the unique, on-chain identifier for their work.
2.  The Creator then proposes a `RoyaltyAgreement` linked to the `ContentMaster`. This agreement specifies initial terms, such as the total royalty pool (100%) and the distribution frequency.
3.  The `RoyaltyAgreement` is established, and the Creator initially holds a 100% `ShareAllocation`.

### 4.2. Managing Share Allocations

1.  The holder of a `ShareAllocation` can propose to split their share and transfer a portion to a new **Rights Holder**.
2.  For example, the Creator with a 100% share can propose to transfer 50% to a co-author.
3.  The co-author must accept the proposal.
4.  Upon acceptance, the Creator's original `ShareAllocation` contract is archived, and two new `ShareAllocation` contracts are created atomically: one for the Creator with 50% and one for the co-author with 50%.
5.  The Daml model ensures that the total percentage of all `ShareAllocation` contracts for a `RoyaltyAgreement` always sums to exactly 100.0. Any transaction that would violate this invariant will fail.

### 4.3. Usage Reporting by Licensees

1.  Before reporting, a **Licensee** must have an active `LicenseeAgreement` for the specific `ContentMaster`. This ensures only authorized parties can submit revenue data.
2.  The Licensee submits a `UsageReport` for a given period (e.g., Q1 2024), detailing the gross revenue generated.
3.  The creation of the `UsageReport` contract signals that a new pool of funds is ready for distribution.

### 4.4. Royalty Distribution and Settlement

This is the core automated workflow of the engine.

1.  **Trigger**: The distribution is triggered when a `UsageReport` is submitted and a corresponding payment is made to the `RoyaltyAgreement`'s settlement account.
2.  **Calculation**: The `RoyaltyAgreement` contract reads the gross revenue from the `UsageReport` and fetches all active `ShareAllocation` contracts associated with it.
3.  **Distribution**: In a single, atomic transaction, the engine calculates the payout for each Rights Holder based on their percentage share.
4.  **Settlement**: The engine executes the payment, transferring the calculated amounts from the central settlement account to each individual Rights Holder's wallet. This is typically done using a CIP-0056 compliant token (e.g., a stablecoin) on the Canton network.
5.  **Atomicity**: The entire distribution is atomic. If any single payment to a Rights Holder fails for any reason, the entire transaction is rolled back. No partial payments are ever made.
6.  **Auditing**: Once the distribution is complete, the `UsageReport` is marked as processed, and an immutable record of the transaction and all its sub-transactions (the individual payments) is recorded on the ledger, visible to all stakeholders.

## 5. Payment Cadence

The `RoyaltyAgreement` defines the payment cadence. Two models are supported:

1.  **Event-Driven**: Distribution is triggered immediately upon the submission of a `UsageReport` by a Licensee. This is the default and preferred model as it allows for real-time royalty flow.
2.  **Periodic (e.g., Quarterly)**: An off-ledger trigger or a designated party (like the Operator or Creator) can initiate the distribution process at the end of a defined period (e.g., on April 1st for Q1). The process would then aggregate all unprocessed `UsageReport` contracts from that period into a single distribution event.

## 6. Business Rules and Invariants

The Daml smart contracts strictly enforce the following rules:

*   **100% Allocation**: The sum of all `ShareAllocation` percentages for a `RoyaltyAgreement` must *always* equal 100.0.
*   **Authorization**: Only parties with a valid `LicenseeAgreement` can submit `UsageReport` contracts.
*   **Immutability**: Once a royalty distribution is executed, the record is final and cannot be altered.
*   **Transparency**: All stakeholders (Creator, Rights Holders) have full visibility into the `RoyaltyAgreement`, their `ShareAllocation`, and all historical distributions.
*   **Atomic Settlement**: Royalty distributions are all-or-nothing. There is no risk of partial payment or settlement failure.
